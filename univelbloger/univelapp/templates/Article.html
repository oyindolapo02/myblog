THE CITYWALLS

Sampling based on field survey and measurement, this article reveals the component feature of the city-wall materials of the Neolithic Liangzhu City-site, then further discusses theirs source associating with XRD analysis, grain size and geochemical elements. The results are as follows: (1) with the grain size, geochemical elements and clay minerals combination’s characteristics similar with Mantou and Huangnikou mounds’, the northern city-wall earth is dominated by clay silt. City-wall earth’s clay minerals’ combination is Illite-Kaolinite-Vermiculite/Mixture of Chlorite and Vermiculite-Smectite. Padded earth’s characteristics in northern city-wall are similar with the forth layer earth’s which widely distribute in this area. The wall-earth’s source, therefore, is deduced from near Mantou and Huangnikou mounds. (2) Mounds like Mantou went through strong oxidant activity because of its high elevation. The raw earth consists of lacustrine-swamp facies and went through reduction activity because of its low elevation, which result in their different minerals’ component and degree of crystallinity, geochemical elements. The Neolithic people in Liangzhu area used the local earth only for the city-wall’s foundation, not to build the city-wall directly. They choose the soil form near mounds like Mantou which was thought more proper to build city-wall. The full-text was published in Chinese with English abstract.

#2. The bulldog

People continue to ask and I am repeating the info as not everyone sees everything posted. Dr. Gail Golab of the American Veterinary Medical Association in conjunction with the CDC answer your most common questions regarding pets and COVID-19 On my Steve Dale’s Pet World Listen HERE as Dr. Gail Golab, Chief Veterinary Officer, Scientific Affairs and Public Policy at the American Veterinary Medical Association explains what we do know and what we still may not be sure of regarding COVID-19 (SARS-CoV-2) and companion animals. Veterinarians deal with corona viruses a lot and have for a very long time, canine and feline corona viruses – which the two species don’t share with one another or share with people. Also, when we get the common cold it’s usually a human corona virus (not to be confused with the novel corona virus which is now impacting the entire planet) and dogs and cats have never gotten colds from humans. But as Dr. Golab notes, this virus is different, and it’s novel; we’re still learning about it. There was a dog tested for the COVID-19 virus in Hong Kong, owned by someone who was suffering from COVID-19. The dog does have a low level of infection. But the dog has not become ill, and has not been able to spread the virus to other dogs or to people. (Important to note: any animal may carry and infection does not equate with infectious).
